Locked Down

Selective Sharing

Provide Minimal Information

How do you approach different platforms and communities?

I create unique aesthetics and personas tailored to each platform or community.

It's About Self-Expression

I create for myself first; audience reaction is secondary to authentic expression.

I Enjoy Experimentation

I like trying different approaches to see how people respond to various expressions of myself.

How do you maintain relationships across digital platforms?

I focus on meaningful interactions with a core group of connections across platforms.

Selective Engagement

I'm thoughtful about which relationships I maintain on which platforms for privacy and clarity.

How do you handle the boundary between your online and offline relationships?

Integrated Approach

I see digital relationships as extensions of my offline connections and treat them similarly.

Context-Based Sharing

I share different aspects of myself with different groups based on our shared interests.

How frequently do you try new platforms or digital spaces?

Constant Explorer

I regularly seek out new platforms and communities to experience different digital cultures.

Curious Curator

I select platforms that allow me to explore specific interests or creative expressions.

When you join a new community or platform, you typically:

Create a Fresh Identity

I enjoy crafting a new persona specific to that community's culture and norms.

Adapt While Connecting

I maintain core aspects of my identity while adapting to connect with new communities.

Protection-Focused (Privacy Guardian)

Primary concern:

Security and privacy

Digital behavior patterns:

  • Minimal sharing of personal information
  • Strong boundaries between platforms
  • Regular privacy audits
  • Selective digital presence

Ideal Personal Digital Twin features:

  • Strong encryption and security protocols
  • Minimal data collection and retention
  • Automatic privacy settings optimization
  • Digital footprint monitoring

Risks to watch for:

  • Digital isolation
  • Missing opportunities for connection
  • Overly restrictive self-presentation
  • Security fatigue leading to lapses

Recommendations:

  • Create tiered privacy protocols allowing selective vulnerability
  • Schedule regular "safe sharing" experiences
  • Develop a privacy-first, not privacy-only approach
  • Use privacy tools that don't compromise user experience
